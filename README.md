# FINANCIAL-SAMPLE-DATASET-ANALYSIS

📖 **Project Overview**  
This project focuses on analyzing financial sample data to uncover sales variations, growth trends, and profitability insights. The dataset provides a foundation for understanding business performance and supports decision-making through interactive dashboards built in Power BI.

🎯 **Project Objectives**  
- Identify sales variations and highlight growth trends  
- Calculate and monitor key financial metrics (sales, profit, margins, discounts)  
- Provide actionable insights for business operations and strategy  
- Build interactive dashboards to support data-driven decision-making  

📂 **Dataset**  
- Records: 1,000+ rows  
- Attributes: Segment, Country, Product, Discount Band, Units Sold, Manufacturing Price, Sale Price, Gross Sales, Discounts, Sales, COGS (Cost of Goods Sold), Profit, Date, Month Number, Month Name, Year  

🛠️ **Tools Used**  
- Excel (data cleaning, preprocessing)  
- Power Query Editor (data transformation)  
- Power BI (dashboard creation, visualization)  
- DAX (custom measures and calculations)  

🧹 **Data Cleaning and Preprocessing**  
Steps performed:  
- Corrected typos and resolved formatting inconsistencies  
- Removed duplicate entries for data integrity  
- Standardized column structures for smooth analysis  

📊 **Analysis & Visualization**  
For visualization, the following techniques were used:  
- Displayed key business metrics (sales, total orders, year-over-year sales, profit, profit margin, discounts) using card visuals  
- Slicers for dynamic exploration  
- Trend charts for year-over-year comparisons  
- Interactive dashboards for stakeholder decision-making  

🔍 **Findings**  
- **Orders by Country:** Canada, France, Germany, Mexico, and USA each recorded **140 orders** → equal distribution across regions.  
- **Sales by Country:** USA had the **highest sales**, followed by Canada and France; Mexico had the **lowest sales**.  
- **Discount Distribution:** High discounts accounted for **57.76%**, medium for **32.62%**, and low for **9.62%** → majority of discounts fall into the high band, impacting profitability.  
- **Sales Trend (2013–2014):** Peak sales in **October 2014**, drop in November, recovery in December → seasonal fluctuations highlight opportunities for targeted campaigns.  
- **Profit Margin by Segment:**  
  - Channel Partners: **73.13%** (strongest contributor)  
  - Midmarket: **27.71%**  
  - Government: **21.69%**  
  - Small Business: **9.77%**  
  - Enterprise: **-3.13%** (negative margin, potential concern)  
  - ✅ Useful insight: highlights which segments drive profitability and which require improvement.  

✅ **Recommendations**  
- Focus on high-margin products and profitable segments (Channel Partners, Midmarket).  
- Reassess discount strategies to protect margins.  
- Target seasonal peaks (October) with planned campaigns.  
- Address underperforming regions (Mexico) and negative-margin segments (Enterprise).  
- Use dashboards for regular performance reviews and strategic planning.
